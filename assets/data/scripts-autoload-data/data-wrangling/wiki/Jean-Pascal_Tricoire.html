Jean-Pascal Tricoire (born 1963) is the CEO and chairman of Schneider Electric, the global specialist of  energy management and industrial automation,  with €25.7bn in revenues in 2018, 137,000+ employees worldwide, and a global presence in over 100 countries.

Early Life and education[edit]

Born in the West of France, Tricoire holds a degree in Electronic Engineering from Ecole Supérieure d'Electronique de l'Ouest and an MBA from EM LYON Business School.

Career[edit]

Jean-Pascal Tricoire spent his early career with Alcatel, Schlumberger and Saint-Gobain, joining Schneider Electric (Merlin Gerin) in 1986.

From 1988 to 1999 he worked in new operation development abroad, in Italy (five years), in China (five years), and then in South Africa.

He transitioned to global positions from 1999 to 2001, building the company transformation program for one year, and based in the US, leading Strategic Global Accounts in North America.

From 2002, Tricoire entered the Schneider Electric executive committee in charge of the International division, which covered all business outside of North America and Europe.

In October 2003, he was appointed Deputy CEO and COO.

In May 2006, he became President and CEO of the management board of Schneider Electric SA.

In May 2013, Tricoire was appointed Chairman and CEO of Schneider Electric, following a change in the governance of the Company,[1] and was re-elected to the position on April 25, 2017.

Over the years, Tricoire transformed Schneider from a transatlantic company to a global group, through organic growth and acquisitions. He moved the company from its position of vendor of electrical products, to a leader of the digital transformation of energy management and industrial automation, providing digital systems,[2] solutions and services thanks to a strong focus on innovation and R&D.[3]

Jean-Pascal Tricoire was named one of the top 100 Top Performing Chief Executives  in 2019 by Harvard Business Review (HBR), ranking at #48[4]. The list analyzes companies that were in the S&P Global 1200, along with financial data, total shareholder return, and change in market capitalization. This is great recognition for Schneider to be included in HBR list this year, based on criteria balancing ESG and financial performance.

Boards & Organizations[edit]

In 2007, Tricoire became Knight of the Order of the Legion of Honor (Chevalier de la Légion d’Honneur).

He is the co-chair of the Sino-French Business Council, created by France President Emmanuel Macron and China President Xi Jinping, in January 2018. Tricoire was President of the France-China Committee for ten years (from 2009 to 2018).

Tricoire sits on China President Xi Jinping's Global CEO Council since 2018 and China Premier Li Keqiang's Global CEO Council since 2014. Tricoire is a member of the International Business Advisory Board of the Mayors of Beijing and Shanghai, and was also a member of the Chong Qing Mayor's International Economic Advisory Council from 2012 to 2015. He was co-chair of the China Development Forum in 2015.

He has been a Member of the Foreign Investment Advisory Council (FIAC)[5] under the Russian government from 2014.

He is Director and co-founder of the Alliance for Societas Europaea Promotion from 2014.

Tricoire is a director of the worldwide board of the UN Global Compact[6] and was President of the Global Compact France from 2014 to 2019.

Tricoire also represents Schneider as a UN HeForShe Corporate IMPACT champion working with other heads of state, universities and companies to advance gender equality. Under his leadership, Schneider has gone from 3% representation of women in leadership 15 years ago, to 20% representation today, with a goal to make that at least 30% by 2020.[7]

Personal[edit]

Jean-Pascal Tricoire is married and has three children.

Tricoire is a fan of whitewater sports and outdoor sports. He loves travelling off the beaten tracks.

He is a strong supporter of sustainable development and is committed to tackling the issues of climate change[8] and gender equality.[9]

Tricoire speaks French, English, Italian and Chinese.[10]
