Phiwa Nkambule (born January 24, 1992) is an internet entrepreneur and businessperson best known for co-founding and leading Riovic as its chief executive officer.[2]

Early life[edit]

Phiwa Nkambule was born in Manzini where he also spent his childhood.[3][4]

In 2006, as a 14-year-old, he began fixing and building computers with his uncle in a small township in Swaziland.[2] Nkambule moved to South Africa in 2007 for academic purposes.[5] Phiwa was a law student at the University of Pretoria before quitting to start his first technology company Cybatar from his garage in 2014.[6] It was at the university where he taught himself web and app development.[2]

Entrepreneurship[edit]

Before leaving the University of Pretoria Phiwa attempted to work with the Swaziland National Library and the Manzini Regional Education Office in 2013 but was rejected.[7] After dropping out of university in 2014 Nkambule founded Cybatar, a technology company focused on fuel delivery from his garage in Pretoria, South Africa.[2][8][9]

In 2015 Nkambule co-founded Riovic, an insurance technology company.[2][4][3][5][7][6] The company owns and operates online platforms that connect consumers with insurance, credit and investments.[10] He was invited to deliver a keynote address on Digital Technologies in Insurance at the 13th International Microinsurance Conference in Peru, South America in November 2017.[3][4][11][10][12]

Philanthropy[edit]

On 24 October 2015 he released the first tuition crowdfunding platform in South Africa in the wake of the FeesMustFall protests that saw the country's tertiary institutions shutdown to help save students from tuition debt.[13][14][15] He also launched a free online learning platform for digital skills aimed at reducing youth unemployment rate in South Africa.[16][17][18]

Awards and recognition[edit]

In June 2018, Phiwa Nkambule was named in the Forbes Africa 30 Under 30 list of most promising entrepreneurs in the technology category.[2] In October he was named in Destiny Man’s Power of 40 list, a list of 40 trailblazers under the age of 40.[19]
